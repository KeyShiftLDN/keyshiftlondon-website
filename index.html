FAQ
Common Questions

frequently asked.

Quick answers on our South London youth development work, practitioner training and coaching.

What is Key Shift London?▾

Key Shift London is a South London youth development Community Interest Company (CIC, reg. 15685849) working with young people aged 11 to 18. We build the precision infrastructure (training, coaching and evidence tools) that turns potential into measurable, lasting outcomes.

What is Precision Training and who is it for?▾

Precision Training is our self-paced online youth practitioner training for parents, mentors, coaches, pastoral leads and youth workers. Level 1 covers the foundations and Level 2 covers live delivery and safeguarding, and each level earns an official certificate of completion. Explore the training.

What is PILLAR coaching?▾

PILLAR is our identity-first youth coaching framework, built around six pillars of practitioner development. You can take the free PILLAR Diagnostic to see which areas to focus on before starting a coaching journey.

What is The Precision Network (TPN)?▾

The Precision Network is the youth development ecosystem that connects young people, mentors, schools, clubs and commissioners through one aligned system, built on identity, accountability, safeguarding and measurable growth. See the network.

Do you work with schools, charities and commissioners?▾

Yes. We partner with schools, youth charities, sports academies and commissioners to deliver youth mentoring programmes and practitioner training, with outcome reporting that funders can trust. Start a conversation.

How much does Precision Training cost?▾

Precision Training Level 1 is £149 and Level 2 is £159 to buy and complete yourself. Facilitator, cohort and organisation-wide licensing options are available on enquiry.
